What about this virus?

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a new disease, caused by a novel (or new) coronavirus that has not previously been seen in humans.
Because it is a new virus, scientists are learning more each day. Although most people who have COVID-19 have mild symptoms, COVID-19 can also cause severe illness and even death.
Some groups, including older adults and people who have certain underlying medical conditions, are at increased risk of severe illness.

How does this virus spread?

COVID-19 is thought to spread mainly through close contact from person to person, including between people who are physically near each other (within about 6 feet).
How easily a virus spreads from person to person can vary.

What are the symptoms of COVID-19?

Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus.
If you have fever, cough, or other symptoms, you might have COVID-19.

Is handwashing enough to kill the virus?

Handwashing is one of the best ways to protect yourself and your family from getting sick.
Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.

Are any common side effects of vaccine?

After getting vaccinated, you may have some side effects, which are normal signs that your body is building protection.
The most common side effects are pain and swelling in the arm where you received the shot. In addition, you may have fever, chills, tiredness, and headache.

How do we protect against COVID-19?

1. Get Vaccinated.
2. Wear a mask.
3. Stay 6 feet away from others.
4. Avoid crowds and poorly ventilated spaces.
5. Wash you hands often.
